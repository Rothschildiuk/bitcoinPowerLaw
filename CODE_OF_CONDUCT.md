# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want the PowerLaw project to be a respectful, constructive, and technically rigorous place for everyone who participates.

Contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to:

- Be respectful in discussions, reviews, and issues.
- Focus on technical arguments, evidence, and reproducible results.
- Assume good intent, but challenge ideas directly and clearly.
- Help keep the project welcoming to people with different backgrounds and experience levels.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that support a healthy community include:

- Giving useful, specific feedback.
- Asking questions in good faith.
- Accepting review comments without hostility.
- Sharing context when proposing changes.
- Respecting different viewpoints and levels of familiarity with the codebase.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory or derogatory language.
- Trolling, baiting, or deliberately inflammatory behavior.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing discussions away from the project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, commits, or other contributions that do not follow this Code of Conduct.

Repeated or severe violations may lead to temporary or permanent bans from project spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the maintainer through GitHub. Please include links, screenshots, or other relevant context where possible.

All reports will be reviewed in good faith and handled as confidentially as practical.
